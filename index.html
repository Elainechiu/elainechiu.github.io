I am a Ph.D. candidate in Statistics at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, advised by Professor Hyunseung Kang. My research focuses on causal inference, sensitivity analysis, and robust statistical inference, developing exact and computationally efficient tools for observational studies with unmeasured confounding.

CV · Email · LinkedIn · GitHub

Research

My research develops new statistical frameworks for sensitivity analysis in observational studies. I focus on extending exact, nonparametric methods to multi-level and cluster-based designs, characterizing robustness through design sensitivity and Bahadur–Rosenbaum efficiency.
